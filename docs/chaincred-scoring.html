How ChainCred
Scores Work

Every wallet gets a single number between 0 and 1000. This document explains every layer of the calculation — from the final score down to individual protocol-level signals and why each decision was made.

Scores measure what wallets do, not what they hold. Depth and breadth of onchain activity across 7 chains, 25 protocols, 35 signals, and 10 sybil heuristics.

Score Formula

The total score combines five weighted category scores, then applies a sybil confidence multiplier.

rawScore = builder.raw × 0.30 + governance.raw × 0.25 + temporal.raw × 0.20 + diversity.raw × 0.15 + complexity.raw × 0.10

totalScore = round(rawScore × sybilMultiplier)

Each category produces a raw score (0–1000) from the sum of its individual signals, capped at 1000. Since the weights add up to 1.0, the raw score naturally ranges 0–1000.

The sybil multiplier (0.0–1.0) is applied last. A clean wallet gets 1.0 (no penalty). A suspicious wallet can be reduced to near-zero.

Design goal: 1000 is nearly impossible — even vitalik.eth scores 967 with real indexed data. Most active users land between 400–800. The score rewards genuine breadth and depth across categories, not just volume.

Builder

30% · 10 signals

Builder activity is the highest-weighted category because deploying contracts requires significant technical skill. A sybil farm doesn't deploy contracts; a developer does. This biases the score toward genuine technical contribution.

SignalFormulaCapWhy
Deployments contracts × 40 280 Core builder signal — each deployed contract shows technical capability
Multi-chain deploys deploy_chains × 50 200 Cross-chain deployment shows deeper DevOps skill and ecosystem commitment
Constructor complexity sqrt(avg_bytes) × 10 100 Large constructor calldata = complex initialization, not simple token clones
Deployment focus (deploys / total_txs) × 400 80 High deploy-to-tx ratio distinguishes dedicated builders from one-time deployers
CREATE2 create2_deploys × 30 120 Deterministic addresses show advanced deployment patterns (upgradeable, counterfactual)
ERC-4337 erc4337_ops × 25 100 Account abstraction usage signals cutting-edge builder activity
Longevity floor(age_months / 6) × 30 90 Points per 6-month period since first deploy — long-term builders > one-time deployers
Verified source verified × 50 200 Publishing source on Etherscan shows commitment to transparency and auditability
External users users × 15 150 Contracts with real users prove utility beyond self-interaction
Active contracts active × 40 200 Contracts still being used, not abandoned — ongoing impact

Cap sum: 1,520 — 52% headroom above the 1000 max. Builders don't need to max every signal.

Signals 8–10 (verified source, external users, active contracts) require Etherscan API enrichment. Without ETHERSCAN_API_KEY, these signals contribute 0.

Temporal

20% · 5 signals

Time-based signals distinguish genuine long-term participants from short-term farmers. An old wallet that survived bear markets is qualitatively different from a new wallet with the same transaction count. Time cannot be faked — you can't backdate transactions.

SignalFormulaCapWhy
Wallet age age_years × 50 400 Each year of age shows the wallet survived through market cycles
Bear market txs bear_txs × 5 300 Transacting during 70%+ drawdown periods shows conviction, not speculation
Consistency (active_months / age_months) × 300 300 Ratio of active months vs. total age — consistent usage beats sporadic bursts
Activity entropy (distinct_hours / 24) × 200 200 Humans transact at 8–15+ distinct hours; bots use 2–3. Anti-automation signal
Cross-cycle persistence bear_periods × 75 300 Surviving multiple distinct bear markets is exceptional commitment

Cap sum: 1,500 — 50% headroom.

Consistency measures behavior pattern, not duration. A 1-year wallet active every month scores the same as a 5-year wallet active every month. Wallet age already rewards duration separately.

Protocol Diversity

15% · 4 signals

Breadth of protocol usage shows genuine exploration rather than single-protocol farming. A wallet that only uses Uniswap is a trader; a wallet that uses Uniswap + Aave + ENS + Safe + Chainlink is a power user.

SignalFormulaCapWhy
Protocol count unique_protocols × 18 350 Raw count of distinct protocols from the 25 in the registry
Chain diversity chains_active × 40 250 Number of chains with activity (7 indexed → effective max 280, capped at 250)
Cross-domain coverage categories × 40 400 Distinct domains touched: defi, governance, infra, social, gaming, builder-tools (6 max → effective max 240)
Early adoption early_adoptions × 30 300 Protocols used within 6 months of launch — early adopters take risk on new protocols

Cap sum: 1,300 — only 30% headroom. The hardest category to max, by design.

Natural scarcity: Chain diversity is bounded by 7 chains (effective max 280, capped at 250). Cross-domain coverage is bounded by 6 categories (effective max 240). Even perfect coverage can't reach 1000 from these two signals alone, forcing genuine protocol count and early adoption.

Complexity

10% · 7 signals

Transaction sophistication separates power users from basic token transfers. Weighted lowest (10%) because volume and complexity can be gamed more easily than other categories, but still provides meaningful signal.

SignalFormulaCapWhy
Transaction volume total_txs × 1.5 300 Base activity count — 200 txs to reach cap
Fail ratio (failed / total) × 1000 300 Higher failure rate = pushing limits. Experienced users hit reverts; bots don't fail
Avg calldata sqrt(total_bytes / txs) × 10 400 Large calldata = complex function calls, not just ETH transfers
Permit permits × 10 200 ERC-2612 permit usage shows DeFi sophistication (gasless approvals)
Flashloan flashloans × 50 300 Flashloan execution is one of the most complex DeFi operations
Smart wallet smart_wallet × 15 150 Using smart contract wallets (Safe, ERC-4337) shows advanced wallet management
Internal txs sqrt(internal_txs) × 8 200 Contract-to-contract calls indicate multi-step operations

Cap sum: 1,850 — 85% headroom, the most generous category. Compensates for the low 10% weight.

Why do failures score higher? A wallet with 10,000 txs and zero failures is likely a bot with perfect simulation. Experienced humans interacting with complex protocols naturally hit reverts (slippage, frontrunning, gas estimation).

Sybil Detection

Every wallet is analyzed by 10 behavioral heuristics. Each detected flag reduces a confidence multiplier multiplicatively. Wallets are never banned — just discounted. All flags are transparent and visible.

confidence = 1.0
for each detected flag:
  confidence ×= (1 − penalty)

Example: Temporal Clustering (0.40) + Action Repetition (0.30):
  1.0 × 0.60 × 0.70 = 0.42 — wallet keeps 42% of raw score
1. Temporal Clustering −40%

New wallets with extreme activity are likely farming. Real users don't sustain 20+ txs/day from day one.

avg_txs_per_day > 20 AND wallet_age < 90 days
2. Action Repetition −30%

Repetitive single-protocol, single-chain activity suggests scripted farming. Real users explore.

total_txs > 100 AND unique_protocols < 3 AND chains_active < 2
3. Funding Graph Clustering −50%

Distributing to 50+ addresses through 1 protocol across chains = distribution bot. Multi-chain requirement excludes gaming users who interact with many addresses on one chain.

unique_recipients > 50 AND unique_protocols < 2 AND chains_active > 1
4. Cross-Chain Mirroring −60%

Using the exact same protocols in the exact same order across 3+ chains is a hallmark of scripted sybil farming that replays the same sequence everywhere.

3+ chains with identical sorted protocol sets (requires chains_active ≥ 3)
5. CEX Withdrawal Freshness up to −30%

Graduated penalty for brand-new wallets. Full penalty at day 0, linearly decays to zero by day 30.

wallet_age < 30 days → penalty = 0.30 × (1 − age/30)
6. Zero Failure Rate −20%

200+ transactions with zero failures suggests perfect simulation — characteristic of bots. Real users inevitably encounter reverts.

total_txs > 200 AND failed_txs = 0
7. Perfect Gas Patterns −15%

Extremely low gas price diversity on mainnet suggests hardcoded gas strategy. L2-only wallets are exempt — L2 gas prices are structurally stable by design.

total_txs > 50 AND distinct_gas / total_txs < 0.5% AND distinct_gas < 10 AND has mainnet activity
8. MEV Bot Activity −15%

Wallets where >30% of activity is MEV-related (sandwich attacks, arbitrage) are bots, not humans.

mev_interactions > 20 AND mev_ratio > 30%
9. Funding Source Cluster −50%

The wallet's funding source also funded 10–500 other wallets — classic sybil coordinator pattern. Below 10 = normal. Above 500 = CEX/bridge/router (exempt).

funding_source_outbound > 10 AND ≤ 500 (requires Etherscan enrichment)
10. CEX Fresh Wallet up to −30%

CEX-funded wallets under 90 days old get graduated penalty. Decays to zero by day 90. Older CEX-funded wallets are fine.

funded_by_cex = true AND age < 90 days → penalty = 0.30 × (1 − age/90)
Design principles: Compound conditions beat single thresholds (each heuristic requires 2–3 conditions). L2s are exempt from gas entropy checks. Funding source uses a range (10–500) because sybils fund moderately while CEXes fund thousands. Graduated penalties decay over time instead of binary cutoffs.

Protocol Registry

ChainCred tracks 25 protocols across 6 domains. Wallet interactions are detected by matching transaction to addresses against the registry's contract addresses per chain.

Selection Criteria

The registry is curated for:

  • Market dominance — the most-used protocol in its category (Uniswap for DEX, Aave for lending)
  • Historical significance — protocols that defined their category (MakerDAO for stablecoins, ENS for identity)
  • Chain representation — at least one protocol per supported chain where possible
  • Domain coverage — ensuring all 6 scoring domains have protocol representation

DeFi Protocols

Uniswap
DeFi ETH · ARB · OP · BASE · zkSync · Polygon

The dominant DEX by volume across all EVM chains. Uniswap invented the AMM model and remains the liquidity backbone of DeFi. Interaction with Uniswap is the single most common DeFi activity. Tracked contracts: V2 Router, V3 SwapRouter02, UniversalRouter.

Launched November 2018
Aave
DeFi ETH · ARB · OP · BASE · zkSync · Polygon

The largest lending protocol by TVL. Using Aave requires understanding supply/borrow mechanics, health factors, and liquidation risk — a meaningful step up from simple swaps. Tracked: V3 Pool.

Launched January 2020
Compound
DeFi ETH · ARB · BASE · Polygon

Pioneer of the lending protocol model. COMP governance token kicked off the "governance token" trend. V3 Comet represents the modern single-asset market architecture. Tracked: V3 Comet (USDC).

Launched September 2018
Lido
DeFi ETH · ARB · OP · BASE · zkSync · Polygon

Dominant liquid staking protocol. Using Lido shows understanding of staking derivatives and DeFi composability (stETH is used as collateral across DeFi). Tracked: stETH, wstETH (mainnet); wstETH bridged (L2s).

Launched December 2020
Curve
DeFi ETH · ARB · OP · BASE · zkSync · Polygon

Launched August 2020
1inch
